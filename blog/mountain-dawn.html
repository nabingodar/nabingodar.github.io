A Mountain Dawn

May 12, 2026 • Nepal

Waking before dawn felt like stepping into a secret. The air was thin and cold, and the peaks held their breath as if waiting. I sat on cold rock with a thermos and watched the world shift from blue to gold. In that quiet, everything else — plans, deadlines, the small urgencies of daily life — receded. There is an economy of attention in those early hours: what remains becomes clear.

Travel compresses time in useful ways. When you remove the familiar scaffolding of home, the small decisions that usually take up space in your head lose their weight. Instead, there is room to notice: the angle of light on a ridge, the sound of a bird at the ridge-line, the way a cup of tea warms your fingers.
