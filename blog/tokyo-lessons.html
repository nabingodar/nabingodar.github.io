Lessons from Tokyo

Nov 3, 2025 • Japan

Tokyo taught me patience in small acts: queuing without complaint, the quiet concentration of people on trains, the slow mastery of a language through daily repetition. Those small disciplines add up into a steady capacity for focus.

When you live somewhere else, you are always translating — words, gestures, expectations. That translation sharpens the sense of what really matters: the ability to listen, the humility to be wrong, and the joy of learning new ways of being.
